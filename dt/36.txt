<P align="left" style="margin-left:8%; font-size: 10pt"><B>Overview</B>



<P align="left" style="margin-left:8%; font-size: 10pt">&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The Corporation had strong first quarter 2004 earnings of
$281&nbsp;million. Earnings from exploration and production were $207
million in the first quarter of 2004, reflecting higher crude oil
and natural gas prices and lower first quarter costs. Refining and
marketing earnings were $112&nbsp;million for the first quarter of
2004, resulting from favorable refining margins and high
utilization rates. The first quarter results may not be indicative
of future operating results.



<P align="left" style="margin-left:8%; font-size: 10pt">&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The
Corporation continues to progress its twelve development projects. The Llano Field is expected to commence production in
the second quarter and the installation of the offshore pipeline
for the joint development area of Malaysia-Thailand is proceeding
as planned. In Equatorial Guinea, the plan of development for the
Northern Block G fields is expected to be submitted to the
government in June.



<P align="left" style="margin-left:8%; font-size: 10pt">&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;With regard to Libya, the United States Government&#146;s easing
of sanctions should allow the Corporation and its partners to
further advance plans to return to our operations there.
Negotiations regarding the terms of re-entry are underway.



<P align="left" style="margin-left:8%; font-size: 10pt"><B>Results of Operations</B>



<P align="left" style="margin-left:8%; font-size: 10pt">&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Net income for the first quarter of 2004 amounted to $281
million compared with $176&nbsp;million in the first quarter of 2003.
Income in the first quarter of 2004 includes a net gain from an

Corporation has 24,000 barrels of oil per day of Brent related

planned production from Equatorial Guinea. A portion of the 2006
through 2012 hedges was transacted subsequent to the quarter end. The
Corporation also has hedged 35% of its remaining 2004 United States
natural gas production at an average price of $5.04 per Mcf. As
market conditions change, the Corporation may adjust its hedge
